Physician Data: What's Available

Publicly Available & Commercial Data on Doctors, Medical Practices, and Physician Purchasing
Prepared by: Sweet Dee (Research Agent)
Date: February 25, 2026
Status: Final
Summary

There is a surprisingly rich ecosystem of publicly available data on U.S. physicians — driven largely by federal transparency mandates passed in the Affordable Care Act era and the Medicare/Medicaid system's need to publish utilization data. The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) alone publishes half a dozen high-value datasets covering NPI numbers, practice addresses, prescribing patterns, procedure volumes, and payments from pharmaceutical companies. Beyond government data, the AMA maintains the most comprehensive private physician registry in the country, and a robust commercial data industry — led by IQVIA, Symphony Health, and Definitive Healthcare — has been built on top of and around these sources to power pharmaceutical sales rep targeting, medical device marketing, and healthcare B2B sales. This report maps the full landscape: what's free, what's licensed, what it covers, and what you can do with it.

1. CMS / Medicare Public Datasets

CMS publishes its data through data.cms.gov and the broader data.gov ecosystem. Most datasets are updated annually and are freely downloadable.

NPPES NPI Registry
FREE
npiregistry.cms.hhs.gov

The foundation of physician data in the U.S. Every healthcare provider who bills Medicare or Medicaid has a unique 10-digit NPI. The full NPPES bulk download (updated weekly) contains: full name, credential, specialty taxonomy codes, primary practice address, phone/fax, organization affiliation, gender, and enumeration date.

Coverage: ~7 million active NPIs. ~1.0–1.2 million active MDs/DOs. Best free starting point for building a physician contact list — name, specialty, address for essentially every practicing physician in the U.S.
Medicare Part D Prescriber Data
FREE
data.cms.gov — Part D Prescribers

Annual dataset. Contains prescribing patterns for physicians who wrote Medicare Part D (outpatient drug) prescriptions. Includes: prescriber NPI, name, specialty, address, drug name (brand and generic), total claims, total 30-day fills, total day supply, total drug cost, and beneficiary count.

The big one for pharma. You can see exactly how many scripts of Drug X a specific physician wrote in a given year. Covers ~1.5 million prescribers. Covers ~40% of U.S. prescriptions. Free, annual, actionable.
Medicare Part B Utilization — Physician & Other Practitioners
FREE
data.cms.gov — Physician Utilization

Claim-level aggregated data on what Medicare Part B physicians billed — by provider and by HCPCS (procedure) code. Covers ~1 million providers. Includes number of services billed, beneficiaries, submitted charges, Medicare allowed amount, and payment amounts.

Key use case: Identifying high-volume proceduralists. A cardiologist billing 2,000 echocardiograms/year is a very different target than one billing 50. This data makes that visible — for free.
Open Payments (Sunshine Act)
FREE
openpaymentsdata.cms.gov

Mandated by the ACA, Open Payments requires drug and device manufacturers to report all payments to physicians. Categories: general payments (speaking fees, consulting, meals, travel), research payments, and ownership interests. Published annually since 2013. $2+ billion in payments reported annually.

KOL identification: Find physicians receiving large consulting or speaking fees from manufacturers. Critical for competitive intelligence — which physicians are your competitors paying, and for what drug/device?
Care Compare / Provider Data Catalog
FREE
data.cms.gov — Provider Characteristics

Quality metrics, board certifications, hospital affiliations, whether physicians accept new Medicare patients, and group practice quality data. Useful for validating physician status, understanding affiliations, and identifying independent vs. health-system-employed practices.

2. AMA Physician Masterfile

AMA Physician Masterfile
LICENSED
ama-assn.org

The gold standard private physician database in the United States. Maintained since 1906. Covers ~1.4 million total records; ~950,000 actively practicing physicians. Includes:

  • Specialty (self-reported + AMA-coded)
  • Practice setting (solo, group, hospital-employed, academic, government)
  • Medical school, graduation year, residency, fellowship
  • Board certification status (sourced from ABMS)
  • DEA number (highly valuable for pharmaceutical targeting)
  • Practice address, phone, email (where available)
  • Hospital affiliations
What makes it different from NPPES: DEA numbers, board certifications, training history, tracks physicians before they have an NPI (residents), and actively maintained currency. Full file license: typically six figures annually for major pharma users.

3. Commercial Data Providers

IQVIA (formerly IMS Health)
LICENSED
iqvia.com

Xponent: The dominant Rx tracking product. Tracks ~80–90% of U.S. retail prescriptions from pharmacy dispensing data. Updated weekly. Prescriber-level, by brand and generic, new-to-brand vs. refill, payer type. This is what pharma sales reps use.

OneKey: IQVIA's physician universe combining AMA Masterfile with their own enrichment — affiliations, digital identifiers, contact info, linked to prescribing data. Industry standard HCP master data solution.

Pricing: Xponent/OneKey combo typically $500K–$5M+/year depending on scope. IQVIA serves all top pharma companies. Revenue ~$15B annually.
Symphony Health (ICON plc)
LICENSED
symphonyhealth.com

ProSource: Physician-level prescription data competing directly with IQVIA Xponent. PatientSource: Patient-level longitudinal claims data (de-identified). Known for better specialty pharmacy and Medicaid coverage. Popular alternative or complement to IQVIA, especially for oncology and rare disease.

Definitive Healthcare
LICENSED
definitivehc.com · NYSE: DH

Leading B2B commercial intelligence for the broader healthcare market. Profiles on ~3.5 million U.S. providers. Includes claims-derived procedure volumes, affiliation mapping, technology usage (which EMR), contact info, and hospital financial data. Pricing: ~$50K–$500K+/year. Primary tool for medical device, health IT, and healthcare services companies.

Veeva OpenData
LICENSED
veeva.com

Physician reference data service integrated natively into Veeva CRM (used by virtually every mid-to-large pharma company). Covers ~2 million U.S. HCPs. Sourced from AMA Masterfile + state licensing boards. Near-real-time updates — when a doctor moves practices, the CRM updates automatically.

Komodo Health
LICENSED
komodohealth.com

330+ million de-identified patient records. Longitudinal claims across all payer types. Strong in rare disease, oncology, and specialty drugs. Competes with IQVIA Real-World Data. Growing fast — used for patient finding, market sizing, and physician targeting via patient journey analysis.

Digital Physician Targeting Networks
LICENSED

Doceree: Programmatic advertising targeting physicians by NPI across medical publisher sites. Doximity Ads: Reach ~80% of U.S. physicians (verified profiles) by specialty, geography, or NPI list. PatientPoint: In-office digital media (waiting rooms, exam rooms).

4. Prescription Data Sources

How Rx Data Actually Works

  1. Pharmacy dispensing: When a prescription is filled, the pharmacy records the drug, quantity, prescriber NPI/DEA, date, and payer.
  2. IQVIA/Symphony aggregate: These companies collect data from ~50,000+ pharmacies covering ~80–90% of U.S. prescriptions, de-identify patient info, attach prescriber identifiers, and sell it as prescriber-level data.
  3. Specialty pharmacy: Separate channel for high-cost biologics, oncology, and rare disease drugs — often licensed separately.

The AMA Opt-Out Issue

The AMA's Physician Data Restriction Program (PDRP) allows physicians to opt out of having their prescribing data used for pharmaceutical sales. The Supreme Court affirmed in Sorrell v. IMS Health (2011) that prescribing data sales are protected commercial speech, but the PDRP opt-out persists. Physicians who opt out are flagged — pharma companies typically cannot use that data for detailing them.

Medicare Part D as a Free Alternative

For companies that can't justify IQVIA costs, CMS Part D Prescriber Data provides genuine physician-level Rx data for the Medicare population — free. Covers ~40% of U.S. prescriptions. Annual, not weekly, and no payer breakdown — but legitimate and actionable for targeting.

6. Data Quality & Coverage Gaps

What's Hard to Get

  • Email addresses: Low coverage in public data. Commercial providers have partial coverage but quality varies. Doximity is the highest-quality channel for verified physician emails.
  • Physician device purchasing: Not publicly available at the physician level. Facility-level purchasing can be inferred from procedure volume data. GPO contracts are private.
  • Independent practice financials: No public source for private physician practice revenue.
  • Real-time data: CMS data is annual (published 18–24 months after reporting). IQVIA Xponent is weekly but expensive.

What's Surprisingly Good

  • The NPI Registry + Part D data combination gives a remarkably detailed picture of prescribing behavior — for free.
  • Open Payments provides genuine financial relationship transparency that the industry treats as sensitive intelligence.
  • Part B procedure volume data allows excellent identification of high-volume proceduralists by specialty.

Emerging Trends

  • NPI as the universal join key: All major datasets now link on NPI — build any physician data system anchored to it.
  • Real-world data explosion: De-identified patient-level claims driving a new generation of physician analytics beyond simple Rx counts.
  • Health system consolidation: 50%+ of U.S. physicians are now health-system-employed — group practice and system-level targeting increasingly important.
